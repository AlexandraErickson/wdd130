Crochet

My mom has known how to crochet for as long as I can remember. When I was in 5th grade she crocheted me a super long red and yellow striped scarf to be my halloween costume, along with a cloak and a magic wand. (You have three guesses what I was.) A couple years after this, she taught me and some girls in the neighborhood how to crochet a chain (the most basic stich.) For a while, we talked every now and again about her teaching me to really crochet, but neither of us were ever able to find the time. Then Covid hit.

My mom was busy working online, but all of a sudden, I was left with nothing but free time. After a few weeks of television and practicing my sewing, I came across an old 'How To Crochet' book for beginners. Since my mom croched, I was able to find a hook and, using some old yarn from my room, I quickly taught myself how to do the basic stiches. It only took me three tries to figure out how to make a square with right angles.

Within a week I was able to make a heart. After two, I made my first granny square. After a month, I had over a dozen, and a year later, I had enough to make a blanket. So I made a blanket. I still have a lot left over.

Anyways, I hope you enjoy!
